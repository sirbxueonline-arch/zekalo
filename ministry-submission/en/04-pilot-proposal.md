## Executive Summary

Zirva is a comprehensive, cloud-based school management platform purpose-built for Azerbaijan's education system, currently live at tryzirva.com and serving schools operating under both the national curriculum and the International Baccalaureate (IB) framework. This document proposes a structured four-month pilot deployment across 5–10 state schools to demonstrate Zirva's institutional readiness, generate rigorous evidence for a ministry-level adoption decision, and identify the precise technical and operational integration requirements that would support a phased national rollout to all 4,700 state schools, 1.8 million students, and approximately 150,000 teachers. The pilot is proposed at no cost to the Ministry or to participating schools. Zirva will provide the full platform, on-site training, dedicated support, and monthly reporting to the Ministry throughout the pilot period. We respectfully request that the Ministry confirm pilot school selection, assign a project coordinator, and provide formal letters of introduction to participating school principals so that onboarding may commence at the earliest mutually agreed date.

## 1. Pilot Objective

### 1.1 Primary Objective

The primary objective of this pilot is to demonstrate, under real operating conditions within the Azerbaijani state education system, that Zirva can function reliably at institutional scale — serving teachers, students, school administrators, and parents simultaneously — and that its feature set meaningfully improves the operational efficiency of school management and the quality of communication between schools and families.

### 1.2 Secondary Objectives

1. **Generate evidence for a ministry-level adoption decision.** The pilot will produce quantitative and qualitative data across all key performance indicators defined in Section 7, enabling the Ministry to make an informed, evidence-based decision on national adoption.

2. **Identify integration requirements with existing MoE information systems.** During the pilot, Zirva's technical team will work alongside the Ministry's IT department to document all existing information systems in use (including e-təhsil and any TQDK-connected systems), assess data exchange requirements, and agree on an API specification for interoperability. This integration work is a prerequisite for full-scale national deployment.

3. **Build institutional capacity.** Training teachers and school administrators during the pilot creates a cohort of experienced Zirva users who can support peer adoption in a broader rollout.

4. **Test resilience in low-connectivity environments.** Rural pilot schools will be specifically used to stress-test offline functionality and low-bandwidth performance, ensuring that a national rollout does not disadvantage schools in underserved regions.

## 2. Proposed Pilot Scope

### 2.1 School Selection Criteria

Zirva proposes a pilot cohort of 5–10 schools selected to represent the diversity of Azerbaijan's state education system. The following geographic and curricular distribution is recommended:

| Category | Number of Schools | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Bakı (urban) | 2–3 | Higher digital literacy baseline; faster onboarding; benchmark data for urban performance |
| Regional city (Gəncə or Sumqayıt) | 2 | Mid-tier infrastructure; representative of the majority of state schools |
| Rural district | 1–2 | Lower connectivity; critical for testing offline and low-bandwidth resilience |

### 2.2 Curriculum Mix

The pilot cohort should include:

- At least 2 schools operating under the Azerbaijan national curriculum
- 1 IB-framework school, if available within the pool of willing participants (Zirva supports both curricula natively)

### 2.3 User Mix

The pilot is designed to serve the following approximate user population:

| User Type | Estimated Count |
|---|---|
| Students | 500–1,500 |
| Teachers | 100–200 |
| Parents / guardians | 500–1,500 |
| School administrators | 10–30 |
| Ministry observers (read-only dashboard access) | 5–10 |

All users will have full access to the Zirva platform at no charge during the pilot period.

## 3. Pilot Duration and Phases

The pilot is structured as a four-month programme divided into three distinct operational phases:

### 3.1 Phase Overview

| Phase | Duration | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| Phase A — Onboarding | Month 1 (Weeks 1–4) | School setup, data migration, user training |
| Phase B — Live Operation | Months 2–3 (Weeks 5–12) | All core features active; real-time support |
| Phase C — Evaluation | Month 4 (Weeks 13–16) | Data collection, surveys, report preparation |

### 3.2 Phase A — Onboarding (Month 1)

During the first month, Zirva's implementation team will:

- Conduct kick-off meetings with each participating school's principal and administrative lead
- Migrate existing student and teacher roster data from the school's current system (paper-based, Excel, or legacy digital system) into Zirva
- Configure school-specific settings: timetables, class structures, subject assignments, grading scales
- Deliver two on-site training sessions per school — one for teachers (focusing on the digital gradebook and attendance module) and one for school administrators (focusing on the dashboard and parent communication tools)
- Activate parent accounts and deliver a parent onboarding guide (print and digital versions in Azerbaijani)
- Conduct a soft-launch verification: confirm all users can log in and access their respective dashboards

### 3.3 Phase B — Live Operation (Months 2–3)

During the live operation phase:

- All core platform features are active: digital gradebook, attendance tracking, real-time parent notifications, school administrator dashboard, Ministry national dashboard, and Zəka AI tutor
- Zirva's support team provides a dedicated WhatsApp group and email channel per school, with a maximum four-hour response time during business hours
- Zirva conducts a mid-pilot check-in (end of Month 2) to present preliminary data to the Ministry and collect structured feedback
- Any platform issues identified during live operation are triaged and resolved within 24 hours for critical issues and 72 hours for non-critical issues

### 3.4 Phase C — Evaluation (Month 4)

The final month is dedicated to:

- Administering teacher and parent satisfaction surveys (NPS and structured questionnaire)
- Extracting and analysing usage data against all success metrics defined in Section 7
- Conducting structured debrief interviews with school principals and at least two teachers per school
- Presenting a final evaluation report to the Ministry, covering all metrics, qualitative findings, identified risks, and a recommended path to scale

## 5. What Zirva Provides Free During the Pilot

Zirva commits to providing the following at no cost to the Ministry, participating schools, or any individual user during the pilot period:

### 5.1 Full Platform Access

All users — students, teachers, parents, school administrators, and Ministry observers — will have unrestricted access to the full Zirva platform for the entire four-month pilot period. No features will be gated or restricted.

### 5.2 On-Site Training

Zirva will deliver a minimum of two on-site training sessions per school:

- **Session 1 — Teacher training:** Digital gradebook, attendance recording, communicating with parents via the platform, accessing the Zəka AI tutor on behalf of students
- **Session 2 — Administrator training:** School dashboard, class and timetable management, accessing and interpreting analytics, exporting data, escalating issues to Zirva support

Additional sessions can be arranged on request at no additional cost during the pilot.

### 5.3 Dedicated Support Channel

Each pilot school will be assigned a dedicated WhatsApp group and email thread monitored by a named Zirva support representative. Response time is guaranteed within four hours during business hours (09:00–18:00, Monday–Friday, Baku time). Critical issues (platform outage or data access failure) are escalated to on-call engineering within one hour.

### 5.4 Zəka AI Tutor — Free for All Students

Zəka, Zirva's AI-powered tutoring assistant, will be available to all students in pilot schools at no charge. Zəka supports interaction in Azerbaijani, English, and Russian, and is aligned to both the national curriculum and IB frameworks. Usage data (session counts, subject distribution, language preference) will be included in pilot reporting.

### 5.5 Monthly Progress Reports to the Ministry

Zirva will submit a structured progress report to the Ministry at the end of each pilot month. Reports will include: active user counts by role, feature utilisation rates, support ticket volume and resolution times, uptime statistics, and a narrative summary of qualitative observations from school visits.

### 5.6 Data Export on Demand

All school data entered into Zirva during the pilot — student grades, attendance records, notification logs — is the property of the school and the Ministry. Zirva will provide data export in standard formats (CSV, JSON) on request at any time during or after the pilot, with no export fees.

## 7. Success Metrics

The following metrics will be tracked throughout the pilot to provide an objective basis for the post-pilot adoption decision.

| Metric | Measurement Method | Target Threshold | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|
| Daily Active Users — Teachers | Platform session logs (unique teacher logins per day) | ≥ 70% of enrolled teachers active on any given school day | Weekly |
| Daily Active Users — Parents | Platform session logs (unique parent logins per day) | ≥ 40% of registered parents active per week | Weekly |
| Grade Entry Rate | % of expected grade entries completed digitally vs. paper workaround | ≥ 80% of all grades entered via Zirva by Month 3 | Monthly |
| Attendance Recording Rate | % of scheduled classes with attendance recorded in Zirva | ≥ 90% of all classes recorded digitally by Month 2 | Weekly |
| Parent Notification Open Rate | Push notification and in-app message open rate | ≥ 60% open rate on notifications | Per notification batch |
| System Uptime | Infrastructure monitoring (Zirva internal + external uptime service) | ≥ 99.5% uptime during school hours | Weekly |
| Average Page Load Time | Real User Monitoring (RUM) — p95 load time | ≤ 3 seconds on a standard mobile connection | Weekly |
| Teacher Time Saved Per Week | End-of-month survey (self-reported, structured question) | ≥ 2 hours per week per teacher by Month 3 | Monthly |
| Zəka AI Student Session Count | Platform session logs for Zəka interactions | ≥ 2 sessions per enrolled student per week | Weekly |
| NPS — Teachers | Post-pilot structured survey (0–10 likelihood to recommend) | Net Promoter Score ≥ 40 | End of Month 4 |
| NPS — Parents | Post-pilot structured survey (0–10 likelihood to recommend) | Net Promoter Score ≥ 45 | End of Month 4 |

Any metric falling below its target threshold in a given measurement period will trigger a written explanation from Zirva to the Ministry within five business days, along with a remediation plan.

## 8. Integration with Existing MoE Information Systems

### 8.1 Current State Documentation

Zirva acknowledges that the Ministry of Science and Education operates existing information infrastructure for managing student data, teacher records, and institutional reporting. During Pilot Phase A (Month 1), Zirva's technical team will conduct a structured discovery session with the Ministry's designated IT contact to document all relevant existing systems, including but not limited to: e-təhsil (the national electronic education platform) and any systems connected to TQDK processes for assessment and examination data. This documentation will serve as the basis for integration planning.

### 8.2 Proposed Data Exchange Framework

Zirva proposes the following approach to interoperability:

- A jointly defined RESTful API specification will be drafted during Month 1 of the pilot, covering the minimum viable data exchange required for full national adoption: student roster synchronisation, grade reporting, attendance reporting, and teacher assignment data
- The API specification will be reviewed and approved by both Zirva's technical team and the Ministry's IT contact before Month 2 begins
- Integration adapters connecting Zirva to existing MoE systems will be built by Zirva's engineering team as a committed deliverable, at no additional cost to the Ministry, as a condition of the transition from pilot to full national adoption

### 8.3 Data Residency

Zirva currently uses Supabase for its database infrastructure. The Ministry's attention is drawn to the fact that Supabase's nearest available region is Seoul, South Korea. Zirva commits to resolving data residency requirements — either by migrating to a data centre within Azerbaijan or the European Economic Area, or by adopting an alternative infrastructure arrangement acceptable to the Ministry — prior to any national-scale deployment. This is identified as a dependency in the scale-up roadmap (Section 12). Zirva will present options and cost implications to the Ministry during the pilot evaluation phase.

## 9. Risk Register

| # | Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Low connectivity in rural pilot schools** — Zirva is primarily a web application; poor internet connectivity may impede usage in rural districts | H | H | Zirva will implement and test offline-mode functionality (service worker caching for grade entry and attendance) before pilot Week 5; rural schools will receive dedicated connectivity testing in Week 2; results will inform offline feature roadmap |
| 2 | **Teacher resistance to change** — Some teachers may be reluctant to abandon familiar paper-based or Excel workflows | H | M | Two on-site training sessions per school; a dedicated WhatsApp support line per school; peer champion programme (1–2 teachers per school designated as Zirva champions); results from similar platform adoptions in comparable education systems will be shared during training |
| 3 | **Data migration errors** — Inaccurate or incomplete roster data from schools may result in incorrect student or teacher accounts | M | H | Data migration will be performed in a staging environment first; school administrators will review and sign off on migrated data before go-live; Zirva will maintain original data exports as a rollback reference |
| 4 | **Low parent smartphone penetration** — In rural areas and lower-income households, parents may not own smartphones capable of running the Zirva parent app | M | M | Zirva's parent interface is optimised for low-end Android devices and basic browser access (no app installation required); SMS notification fallback will be configured for parents without smartphones |
| 5 | **Ministry IT policy restrictions** — The Ministry may have network-level restrictions (firewalls, whitelisted domains) that block access to Zirva from within school networks | M | H | MoE IT contact identification (Section 6) will be used to conduct a network compatibility check in Week 1; Zirva will provide domain and IP whitelisting requirements in advance |
| 6 | **Data residency compliance** — Supabase Seoul region may not satisfy Azerbaijan data sovereignty requirements | M | H | Pilot data will be treated as non-sensitive test data; Zirva commits to presenting a data residency migration plan by Week 8; national deployment will not proceed without resolution |
| 7 | **AI content appropriateness (Zəka)** — Zəka AI tutor outputs may occasionally be inaccurate or culturally inappropriate for Azerbaijani students | M | M | Zəka outputs are filtered through a content moderation layer; all outputs are logged and reviewable; teachers can flag any problematic response directly within the platform; Zirva will review all flagged outputs within 24 hours |
| 8 | **Support capacity for simultaneous school onboarding** — Onboarding 5–10 schools simultaneously may strain Zirva's current support team | M | M | Zirva will assign dedicated implementation managers per school (maximum 3 schools per manager); a structured onboarding checklist reduces variability; escalation path to Zirva engineering for technical issues |
| 9 | **Principal or administrative staff turnover** — Key contacts at a pilot school may change during the four-month pilot | L | M | All training materials will be documented and stored in a school-specific shared folder; Zirva's support channel is accessible to any new staff member with the school administrator login |
| 10 | **Negative pilot outcome affecting adoption decision** — If the pilot produces results below defined thresholds, the Ministry may decline national adoption | L | H | Zirva views a rigorous, honest evaluation as essential to long-term trust; metrics thresholds are set to be achievable but meaningful; Zirva's team will work proactively throughout the pilot to address any underperformance |

## 11. Evaluation Methodology

### 11.1 Data Collection

Pilot evaluation will draw on three data sources:

1. **Platform analytics:** All quantitative metrics (active users, grade entry rates, attendance recording rates, notification open rates, uptime, page load times, Zəka session counts) will be extracted directly from Zirva's platform logs. Data will be collected automatically and continuously, and aggregated weekly for internal monitoring and monthly for Ministry reporting.

2. **Structured surveys:** Teacher and parent satisfaction surveys will be administered at the end of Month 2 (mid-pilot check) and Month 4 (final evaluation). Surveys will be delivered in Azerbaijani via the Zirva platform and, where necessary, on paper for participants with limited digital access. The survey instrument will include NPS questions and structured questions on time saved, ease of use, and perceived impact on communication with parents or the school.

3. **Qualitative interviews:** Zirva's implementation team will conduct structured 30-minute debrief interviews with the principal and at least two teachers from each pilot school at the end of Month 4. A semi-structured interview guide will be used to ensure comparability across schools. Notes will be included in the final evaluation report.

### 11.2 Independence of Evaluation

Zirva acknowledges that the evaluation is conducted by the platform provider, which creates a potential conflict of interest. To mitigate this:

- All raw data will be made available to the Ministry upon request
- The Ministry is invited to appoint an independent observer (e.g., a representative of the Education Institute or an academic institution) to review the evaluation methodology and data
- All survey instruments will be shared with the Ministry before administration
- The final evaluation report will include a section explicitly documenting any metrics that fell below target thresholds and the reasons identified

## 12. Post-Pilot Scale-Up Roadmap

### 12.1 Phase Overview

| Phase | Scope | Timeline | Key Dependencies |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Phase 1 — Pilot** | 5–10 schools; ~500–1,500 students | Months 1–4 (current proposal) | Ministry designation; school participation |
| **Phase 2 — Regional Rollout** | 50–100 schools; ~25,000–50,000 students | 6 months after pilot completion | Positive pilot evaluation; Ministry formal adoption decision; data residency plan agreed |
| **Phase 3 — National Rollout** | All 4,700 state schools; 1.8M students; ~150,000 teachers | 12–18 months after pilot completion | Data residency resolution; MoE system integration complete; national procurement process; Ministry formal contract |

### 12.2 Scale-Up Dependencies

The transition from pilot to regional and national rollout is conditional on the following being resolved:

1. **Data residency:** Zirva must operate from a data centre meeting Azerbaijan's data sovereignty requirements before handling national-scale student data
2. **MoE system integration:** The API integration adapters for e-təhsil and other relevant MoE systems must be built, tested, and approved
3. **Ministry formal adoption decision:** A signed agreement between Zirva and the Ministry covering terms of national deployment, pricing, data governance, and service levels
4. **Infrastructure scaling:** Zirva's infrastructure must be independently verified as capable of handling 1.8 million concurrent or peak users before national go-live

### 12.3 Indicative Pricing

The pilot is fully funded by Zirva at no cost to the Ministry or to participating schools. Post-pilot pricing for national deployment will be subject to negotiation with the Ministry and will take into account the scale of deployment, the Ministry's budget parameters, and the commercial norms applicable to national government contracts. Zirva is prepared to discuss indicative pricing during the pilot evaluation phase and welcomes a dedicated commercial conversation at the Ministry's convenience.
